Confirm a person with significant control over a company

A person with significant control (PSC) is someone who owns or controls your company. They’re sometimes called ‘beneficial owners’.

You must identify your PSCs and confirm who they are within 14 days of the verification statement due date. The PSC might be you, or someone associated with your company. A company can have one or more PSCs.

Who is a PSC?

An individual or company that has at least one of the following:

  • more than 25% of shares in the company
  • more than 25% of voting rights in the company
  • the right to appoint or remove the majority of the board of directors

Who can use this service?

You can use this service if you are a PSC or you are doing this on behalf of someone who is a PSC and you are authorised to use their Companies House personal code.

What you’ll need

To complete this task, you’ll need to have:

  • The relevant company registration number, if our records return more than one matching result
  • The Companies House personal code for each PSC (if confirming someone else)
