Provide verification details for a PSC or relevant legal entity

Use this service to provide the identity verification details for a company’s person with significant control (PSC) or relevant legal entity (RLE). This needs to be done within 14 days of the anniversary of the company’s incorporation date. For newly appointed PSCs who are RLEs this has to be done within 28 days of the appointment.

What is a PSC

A PSC owns or controls a company. They’re sometimes called ‘beneficial owners’. A PSC has at least one of the following:

  • more than 25% of shares in the company
  • more than 25% of voting rights in the company
  • the right to appoint or remove the majority of the board of directors

What is a RLE

A corporate body or firm that owns or controls a company can also be a ‘person with significant control’ (PSC) known as a relevant legal entity (RLE). RLE status is determined by a number of different criteria including voting rights, rights to share in surplus assets, or the right to appoint or remove the members of the company.

What is a relevant officer?

'Relevant legal entities (RLEs) are now required to provide information about a relevant officer. A relevant officer needs to be:

  • a director of the entity, or someone whose roles and responsibilities correspond to that of a company director
  • aged 16 years or over
  • have a personal code from verifying

Who can use this service

This service is for individuals who are:

  • new PSCs of a new or existing company
  • existing PSCs
  • providing verified details on behalf of someone else who is a PSC or a relevant officer of an RLE (provided they are also employees of the RLE)

What you'll need

To complete this task for each PSC or relevant officer, you will need to:

  • verify your identity, if you haven’t already
  • have the Companies House personal code for each PSC and relevant officier of the RLE (if confirming someone else)
  • the company registration number
What is a Companies House personal code?
This is a unique code that is received once a person's identity is verified with Companies House.
